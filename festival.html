Marbat Festival

  1. Unique to Nagpur: Marbat is a traditional festival celebrated mainly in Nagpur, Maharashtra, during the Shravan month (August-September).
  2. Effigies Procession: Large effigies of evil spirits (Marbat) — mainly Kala Marbat and Pivli Marbat — are paraded through the city and later burnt to drive away negativity.
  3. Social Commentary: The effigies often represent social or political evils, and people also make "badgyas" (mock effigies) of current controversial figures or issues.
  4. Community Involvement: Various local groups participate in making and parading these effigies with dhol, dance, and slogans, creating a vibrant atmosphere.
  5. Cultural Heritage: The festival is over 100 years old, reflecting Nagpur's folk culture, and symbolizes the victory of good over evil.

Makar Sankranti Festival

  1. Harvest Festival: Makar Sankranti marks the end of winter and the beginning of the harvest season, celebrated with enthusiasm across Nagpur.
  2. Tilgul Tradition: People exchange tilgul (sesame and jaggery sweets) saying "Tilgul ghya, god god bola", promoting unity and sweetness in relationships.
  3. Kite Flying: The skies of Nagpur are filled with colorful kites, especially flown by children and youth from rooftops.
  4. Cultural Programs: Schools and local communities organize dance, music, and rangoli competitions to celebrate the day.
  5. Special Food: Traditional dishes like til ladoos, puran poli, and gulachi poli are prepared and shared among family and friends.

Ganesh Chaturthi Festival

  1. Grand Celebrations: Ganesh Chaturthi is celebrated with great devotion in Nagpur, with both household and public Ganpati installations.
  2. Beautiful Idols: Artists create stunning Lord Ganesha idols, which are worshipped with daily aartis, bhajans, and offerings.
  3. Public Mandals: Various local groups and mandals set up decorated pandals and organize cultural events, competitions, and social drives..
  4. Community Participation: The festival encourages unity and devotion, with people from all communities joining the celebrations.
  5. Visarjan Procession: On the final day, Ganpati Visarjan is done in lakes or artificial tanks with lively processions of music, dance, and chants of "Ganpati Bappa Morya!"
