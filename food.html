Tarri Poha

  1. Nagpur Special: Tarri Poha is a signature breakfast dish that originated and gained popularity in Nagpur.
  2. Spicy Tarri Gravy: It’s served with a spicy chana-based gravy called Tarri, poured over soft, steamed poha (flattened rice).
  3. Popular Street Food: Found widely at street stalls and local eateries, especially in areas like Sitabuldi, Sadar, and Mahal.
  4. Topped with Sev & Lemon: Garnished with farsan/sev, chopped onions, coriander, and lemon juice for extra flavor and crunch.
  5. Budget-Friendly & Filling: Loved for being affordable, tasty, and filling, making it a favorite among students and locals alike.

Orange Barfi

  1. Nagpur’s Sweet Specialty: Orange Barfi is a unique sweet delicacy made using Nagpur’s famous oranges.
  2. Tangy and Sweet Flavor: It has a refreshing citrus taste blended with traditional khoya (milk solids) and sugar.
  3. Attractive Appearance: The barfi is usually orange in color, cut into squares or diamonds, and sometimes topped with silver vark.
  4. Popular Gift Item: Often used as a festive gift during Diwali and other celebrations due to its distinct regional identity.
  5. Available at Famous Sweet Shops: You can find the best Orange Barfi at well-known sweet shops in Nagpur like Haldiram’s and Shree Heera Sweets.

Kulhad Chaat

  1. Served in Clay Cups: Kulhad Chaat is a flavorful street food served in eco-friendly clay cups (kulhads) that add an earthy aroma.
  2. Spicy and Tangy Mix: It includes a mix of boiled chana, potatoes, onions, spices, curd, and chutneys, creating a spicy-tangy taste.
  3. Popular Street Snack: Widely available at street stalls and local chaat corners, especially in areas like Sadar, Dharampeth, and Sitabuldi.
  4. Topped with Sev and Pomegranate: Often garnished with sev, coriander, and pomegranate seeds, making it colorful and crunchy.
  5. Hot and Fresh: Unlike regular chaat, it is usually served warm, making it a unique and comforting treat for food lovers.
